# The Career Spark — Evidence Without Portfolio

How to extract proof when the candidate has no published work, no portfolio, no GitHub. The career itself is the evidence.

---

## Where to Find Evidence

### 1. Public and Semi-Public Work

Campaigns, product launches, events, press releases. The candidate may not think of these because "that was just my job." Press coverage (even trade press) is third-party evidence. Client outcomes (sales improvement, customer satisfaction) are evidence of the candidate's contribution. Industry awards, even "shortlisted for," count.

### 2. Social Proof

**LinkedIn recommendations** capture skills the candidate would never claim. "She was the person we called when the project was in trouble" IS evidence of fixer skills. Performance reviews, emails, Slack messages where others described the candidate's contribution. If the candidate was repeatedly recruited by former colleagues, the reputation-driven career IS the portfolio.

### 3. Metrics and Outcomes

Volume/scale: "10,000 orders per month," "team of 15." Before/after: "How many errors before? How many after?" Cost savings, efficiency gains: "Reduced processing time by 30%." Revenue impact, even approximate: "client retention 20% above team average."

### 4. Stories as Evidence

When metrics are unavailable, specific stories become proof. Solicit actively:

- **Crisis:** "A time something went seriously wrong and what you did." → diagnostic ability, composure, problem-solving
- **Improvement:** "Something not working well and how you made it better." → initiative, process thinking
- **People:** "Someone you helped grow." → mentoring, team impact
- **Constraint:** "Accomplishing something with fewer resources than needed." → resourcefulness, prioritization
- **Disagreement:** "A time you disagreed with a decision." → communication, judgment, professionalism

### 5. The Career Itself

**Longevity** = relationships, adaptability, sustained value. **Progression** = people who saw daily work chose to give more. **Being the go-to person** = domain expertise and trust without matching title. **Surviving organizational change** = multiple decision-makers concluded too valuable to lose.

---

## Framing Evidence Without Portfolio

Three layers per role:
1. What the candidate was responsible for (scope, scale)
2. What they specifically did (actions, decisions)
3. What changed as a result (outcomes, impact)

If layer 3 is hard to quantify, use layer 2 at higher specificity. The specific action is the proof; the metric is a bonus.

Avoid "responsible for." It describes a job description, not evidence:
- "Managed" → "Ran a portfolio of 40 accounts, maintaining 95% retention"
- "Responsible for training" → "Built the onboarding program used by all new hires across three offices"
- "Worked on the migration" → "Designed the data mapping that resolved the 6-month backlog"

## Turning Daily Work Into Evidence

### "What did you actually do today?"

Not the job description. The actual day. Evidence hides in the details:
- "I open tickets and prioritize them" → triage, decision-making under uncertainty
- "I talk to upset clients" → de-escalation, customer retention
- "I train new people" → knowledge transfer, curriculum design
- "I make sure numbers match" → data integrity, error detection
- "I coordinate warehouse and office" → cross-functional communication, logistics
- "I handle the schedule when people call in sick" → resource allocation under constraint

### "What breaks when you're on vacation?"

Reveals the candidate's unique contribution. Things only they do, that the organization depends on without realizing.

### "What did you change?"

Almost every worker improves something. A shortcut, a template, a better process. These improvements are evidence of initiative even if unrecognized.

## Industry-Specific Evidence

**Healthcare:** Patient outcomes, certifications, protocols improved, training delivered, committee participation, incident response.

**Education:** Student outcomes, curriculum developed, programs created, parent engagement, grants, technology integration.

**Trades:** Certifications/licenses (these ARE the portfolio), complex jobs solved, safety record, apprentices trained, efficiency improvements.

**Retail/hospitality:** Store metrics, team size, training programs, crisis handling, process improvements, customer retention.

**Administrative:** Volume handled, systems managed, process improvements, events organized, onboarding delivered, "go-to person" evidence.

---

## When There Really Is Nothing

The skill cannot manufacture evidence. For truly thin resumes, shift to advisory: what can they build in 3–6 months?

- Charity/volunteer work, pro bono for nonprofits
- Personal projects with tangible outputs (blog, app, newsletter)
- Open-source contributions (even documentation)
- Online courses with portfolio components (the deliverable, not the certificate)
- Community involvement (coaching, tutoring, organizing)
- Competitions, hackathons, professional association participation
- Part-time work reframed: a restaurant night shift = prioritization, team coordination, pressure management

Help the candidate identify which activities build the strongest evidence for their specific target roles.

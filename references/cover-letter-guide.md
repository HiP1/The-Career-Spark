# The Career Spark — Cover Letter Guide

Cover letters make arguments, not introductions.

---

## 1. Structure

1. **Opening sentence.** Single strongest argument. If the reader stops here, they know the pitch.
2. **Evidence.** Career stories, portfolio, metrics. 2–3 sentences max.
3. **Gap acknowledgment.** Name gaps before they become objections. Each immediately followed by reframe.
4. **Close.** What the candidate brings, logistics as selling points.

250–300 words maximum.

---

## 2. Opening Sentence Patterns

**Direct match:** "I have independently designed the product you are hiring someone to build."

**Transferable insight:** "You need pattern recognition applied to data you already have. The domain is learnable. The skill is not."

**Velocity signal:** "In one month with Claude, I produced: [tangible outputs]."

**Structural argument:** "Your three open roles solve the same problem at different altitudes. I can see the full stack."

**Honest broker:** "I have spent 14 years watching a parallel industry build trust infrastructure that failed. I know where the failure modes are."

Never open with: "I am excited to apply for..." / "With over X years..." / "I believe I would be a great fit..."

---

## 3. Gap-Naming Framework

Pattern: "I do not have [gap]. [Reframe as context, not limitation.]"

Examples:
- "I do not have a completed degree. My education is 27 years of building systems at technological frontiers."
- "I do not have formal PM titles. I have 14 years of product decisions made as CTO."

Name gaps the reader will notice in 30 seconds. Do not name gaps they would not notice. Never name a gap without the reframe.

---

## 4. IP Protection Strategy

**Candidate's ideas compete with company's products:** "AI Product Architectures (unpublished). Details available in interview." Signal capability without delivering value.

**Candidate's ideas enhance company's products:** Describe the shape, withhold the mechanism. "I have designed solutions for the blank-page problem" is safe. Specific mechanisms risk cryptomnesia.

**The "deliver the brief + deliver better" principle:** Deliver what they asked for (the standard application that meets stated requirements) PLUS what they did not know they needed (an insight, a reframing, a connection they missed). The standard application is the safe option. The additional insight is the bonus that gets attention. Let them choose which to lead with. Named after the production methodology where the team always delivered what the client requested AND a version that exceeded the brief.

---

## 5. Logistics as Selling Points

"No visa required. No relocation required. Immediate start." = three cost centers eliminated. Frame from the company's cost perspective.

---

## 6. Cross-Role Awareness

"I am applying for role X, but I notice roles Y and Z address the same challenge at different altitudes." Demonstrates strategic thinking above individual role level.

---

## 7. Per-Variant Reframing

Same CTO experience, four honest framings:

| Target Role | Framing |
|-------------|---------|
| Information Quality | "Information economics operations: every impression a pricing signal, every click a quality judgment" |
| Product PM | "Audience engagement optimization: user interaction quality determined business outcomes" |
| Strategy | "Strategic pattern recognition across five technology shifts before they had names" |
| Consumer PM | "Consumer founder obsessed with craft: 14 years where the standard was above client expectation" |

Portfolio protection is per-company: at Company A (designs compete) → "details in interview." At Company B (designs enhance) → describe more openly.

---

## 8. Style

Apply the candidate's writing style. The cover letter is a writing sample. Front-load conclusions. Match the company's register: startup (direct, personal), enterprise (formal, evidence-heavy), research lab (methodology-aware, epistemic humility).

Do not give the answer for free. "I have designed a product architecture that addresses this problem. I would prefer to discuss it in person." gets the interview. Three paragraphs of free consulting does not.
